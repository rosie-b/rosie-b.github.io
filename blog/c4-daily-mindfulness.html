#9 Daily Mindfulness

Seach Inside Yourself (chapter 3)

Mindfulness Conversation

I completed the informal exercise with my sister and found setting a timer helped us to be able to focus fully on the each other. I learned that in the usual flow of conversation, we take turns very regularly, so at times it felt uncomfortable to keep my mouth shut. The overall feeling was that the amount of information we shared was the same as per our normal conversations. However, the feeling of making a point to give the other person our undevided attention felt like quite a nice gesture.

My sister felt it took a bit to get used to and it took a bit of effort to think of things to continue to talk about, when not getting input from the other person.

Both of us noticed that we do this type of exercise on a fairly regular basis with our small children, listening intently to their stories and (at times) ramblings. Although, perhaps in shorter bursts of time than in this exercise.

Sustaining Practice

The technique that I'll be using to sustain my practice is "Do less than you can". I have already been using this just out of a natural inclination, finding that if I limited myself to less time than I knew I could do, it left me wanting more.

Other Musings

As with other areas of the EDA experience, trusting the process of meditation is something that keeps on coming up time and time again. It will get easier and come more naturally.

Also, I loved this part about mindful listening:

"If there are people in your life you care about, be sure to give them a few minutes of your full attention every day. They will blossom like flowers."
