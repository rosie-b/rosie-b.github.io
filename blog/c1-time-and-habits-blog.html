Time & Habits

Which timebox and habits techniques did you try?

I have been trying the "4 questions to ask yourself each day", from earlier resources/articles on the concept. This is to help me build awareness of what I'm trying to achieve, what I think I'm able to achieve today and then what I'm actually able to achieve in the time I have available. The questions are:

    Start of day
  • What are my goals today?
  • What are my challenges today?
    End of day
  • Have I reached my goals today?
  • What have I learned today?
I have also been paying closer attention to myself and when my mind wonders, bringing it back on task. If that's not working, I'll stop and get a cuppa.

What did you find interesting?

A lot of the materials were familiar, as I've been starting to explore this area in recent months. I find the fixed vs growth mindset particularly interesting. Since reading the book, it's been really fascinating exploring where my headspace is at in a current moment/on a current task (particularly if I'm feeling challenged or stressed out). And in reflecting on past situations, analysing where I may have been at and what impact this had.

What worked for you?

Setting daily tasks and reflecting on these has been great for keeping me on task, having tangible achievements each day and helping me improve on my estimation skills.

Which will you experiment further with?

I will be experimenting with the Pomodoro technique. I'll also be delving more into meditation techniques, as this is something I've found challenging and want to improve on.
