Meditation

Seach Inside Yourself (chapter 2)

Before reading this chapter, I found that my thoughts on meditation were still lingering around trying to think of "nothing" while meditating. Given that my mind NEVER stops thinking (often multiple things at once), this is something I've always found to be a real blocker in trying to meditate.

After reading this chapter, I've allowed myself to instead think of one thing for the duration of the meditation. That may be my breathing, a specific sound or an image. Also, while in the meditation, it's OK for thoughts, feelings, sensations to creep in. Just observe/acknowledge and then continue on with the mediation.

I really like the technique of:

  • Acknowledge
  • Experience without judging
  • React if you need to
  • Let it go
I've been using this in my recent meditations and feel that it helps to reduce the struggle I would normally experience ("No! Stop thinking about other things! Oh no, now I'm thinking about not thinking about other things! Argh now I'm thinking about cat gifs!" And so on, you get the picture.).

Another aspect I quite liked was the idea of entering into a meditation with a specific intention. I haven't tried this one yet, but think it could be an interesting habit to get into.
