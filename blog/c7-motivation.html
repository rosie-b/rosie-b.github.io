Search Inside Yourself (chapter 6)

Pleasure, Passion and Purpose

Chapter 6 talks about there being three different types of happiness, with varying levels of sustainability:

  1. Pleasure - The least sustainable form of happiness, this high tends be fairly fleeting. We also have a tendency to adjust to this high, thus reducing it's impact and sending us on our next mission to find that feeling again.
  2. Passion - This is a more sustainable form of happiness and resides in those every day activities that spin your wheels. Times where you get lost in an activity and wonder where the hours went. This can be weaved into daily life and if you can incorporate this into your job - fantastic!.
  3. Higher Purpose - Considered to have the most impact on wellbeing and happiness, as well as being the most sustainable. This is where you are part of something bigger than yourself, something that aligns with your values and where you feel that what you're doing has real meaning.

    4. I agree with the argument that we spend most of our time seeking pleasure, when we should focus on finding our purpose for sustainable happiness instead.

    'Discovering values and higher purpose' exercise

    My core values and what I stand for are around being kind, honest and authentic, fairness, looking always to imporove on the status quo and assuming the best in people/situations. My family and friends are really important to me, so their welfare and happiness is something I consider often.

    If my day to day life is out of alignment with these core values, I feel particularly uncomfortable.

    Resilience ideas and meditation exercise

    I struggled with the meditation exercise, having trouble bringing to mind specific events. It's something I will try to be more aware of when future experiences arise. This way I can monitor my genuine reaction, in the moment.

    Other musings

    The activity around envisioning where you want to be and talking about it a lot with people is something I really liked.
